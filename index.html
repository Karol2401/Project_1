What is yoga?

We associate yoga with tranquillity, gentle and specific movements and relaxing music. However, this extraordinary activity is much deeper and more important than you might think. It develops movement capabilities, excellent relaxation and tranquillity, and a host of other benefits.

History

Yoga is an ancient practice that has evolved over the centuries. Today, when we talk about yoga, we think of a type of subtle gymnastics in which we move the body in a series of easy and more difficult movements. Going back to the beginnings of yoga, we will find that there are a few types of yoga, not always practised in the same way....

The origins of yoga are related to the Indus-Saraswati civilisation and archaeological finds presenting figures in yoga postures. The original ideas of yoga, however, appear in the oldest Indian texts, the Vedas, considered by Hindus to be revealed texts and the basis of Brahmanism, the original Hindu religion, which in time evolved into Hinduism.

Types of yoga practiced over the centuries: Jain yoga, Bhakti yoga, Karma yoga, Kundalini yoga, Hatha yoga, Buddhist yoga, Classical yoga, Tantric yoga, Hatha yoga, Laya, Kundalini yoga
